Coordination underneath. Judgment stays with you.

Marrymap exists for couples who skipped the $5,000 planner but still need someone running the logistics — without taking the decisions away from the two people who actually care.

The problem we saw

Every wedding checklist app organizes tasks. None of them help with judgment — whether a photographer earns the price, when invitations should go out, or if a quote is fair.

Couples doing this for the first time inherit ~200 decisions across six months, with no benchmark for what's normal and no one flagging when the budget is about to break.

What we built

Marrymap generates a full planning timeline from the wedding date and venue, then breaks it into weekly steps. Vendor emails draft in the couple's voice. Quotes compare side by side. Budget tracking connects to bank data.

One dashboard shows what's done, what comes next, and what sits at risk — so couples approve and adjust while coordination runs underneath.

Our roadmap

First ten couples reveal which decisions spike the sharpest anxiety — and those automate first. Everything else waits until core planning proves out.
